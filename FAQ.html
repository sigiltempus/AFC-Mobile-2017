Frequently Asked Questions
Posted on Feb 13, 2016 in: FAQ

Here are some answers to the questions we've recieved most often...

1. Where will the team be based (i.e., what is the home field)?

AFC Mobile will play its inaugural season at Herndon (Sage) Park (Dauphin Street & Sage Avenue) in Mobile.

2. Who will manage the team in the first year?

We are actively pursuing a top-class manager, and are approaching several candidates with both high school and college coaching experience in and around Mobile. It is important to us that our coaching candidate has a USSF C License or the NSCAA equivalent.

3. How will players be recruited and selected?

Players will be selected to play for AFC Mobile in both an open and invitational tryout.

4. What type of organization is this (i.e., LLC, 501(c)3, etc.)?

AFC Mobile is in the process of forming an LLC, which will be the Association Football Club of Mobile, LLC.

5. Why $5000?

There are numerous fees in getting a team set up and ready for the upcoming season, including league fees, uniforms, game balls, referee fees, travel costs and many others. Your donation and/or sponsorship would help us out tremendously! We couldn’t do it without the fans and community who share the love and the passion of soccer like we do.

6. Who are the board members?

Our board is currently made of of 10 members

  • Abram Chamberlain – President
  • Sean Landry – CFO
  • Mitchell Kahalley– Director of Communications
  • Daniel McMillan– Director of Digital Communications
  • Stephanie Ward– At Large Member
  • Jeff Garl – Member At Large

  • Zack Taylor – Member At Large
  • Michael Lynch – Member At Large
  • Jeff Moody - Member At Large

7. Are there other organizations like AFC Mobile that have succeeded?

AFC Mobile is following in the tradition of American clubs like Grand Rapids FC, Nashville FC, Indy Eleven, the Birmingham Hammers and other grassroots-based teams. We started as nothing more than a group of fans who followed local soccer and wanted to give people the chance to see the beautiful game played a high level in Mobile as well as represent the city across the Gulf Coast and showcase local players and coaches.

8. What league do you plan to play in?

AFC Mobile will play in the Gulf Coast Premier League, a USASA Elite League. The GCPL is a division 5 league in the United States Soccer Federation (USSF) structure. The top teams in the GCPL will qualify for the Lamar Hunt U.S. Open Cup, the knockout competition that matches up teams from every league in the United States.

9. How do I get involved?

There are several ways you can support AFC Mobile. If you’d like to support us financially, you can donate to our GoFundMe at (gofundme.com/afcmobile). You can also support us and represent your city by buying our merchandise from our online store. You can support the club by sharing our information on social media, or by word of mouth.

Information about season tickets and schedules will be announced soon. In the meantime, if you have questions, you can reach out to information@afcmobile.net

10. What sponsorship opportunities are available?

Sponsorship opportunities are available. More information on sponsorships can be found at http://afcmobile.net/sponsors.html
